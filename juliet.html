Who am I?

I am an Engineering student at Duke University. I am from Austin, Texas so I enjoy cooking Tex Mex, baking, running, live music and going out on the lake.

Putting all of these together, my favorite day would be, after running in the morning, to go out on the lake and wakesurf with great music playing, then to come home and make chips and guac for everyone. Another thing, objectively, I make the best guacamole and ~partially~ make the best chips in the world. First off, guac has to be chunky. You have to be able to visually see and feel in your mouth the tomatoes, onions and cilantro. However, you don't want to be able to see the serrano peppers--these must be chopped fine so that you get a little flavor in every bite. Personally, I’m a spicy food lover so I like to leave in a lot of the seeds. If I’m cooking for a big group though, there are most likely going to be weiners in the crowd who hate spiciness, so I’ll remove the seeds. However, these people don't know how strenuous it is to remove the seeds. While scraping them out, I almost always get the serrano chile oil in the crevices between my fingers, which lingers with a slight burning feeling for the next two days.

So, the ingredients I use are ripe avocados, tomatoes with the juices squirted out, extra onions, finely chopped cilantro, serrao peppers (finely chopped with or without seeds) and salt. Lately, I have been persuaded to add lime, but I am extremely cautious due to the number of store-bought guacs I have tried that are quite literally swimming in lime juice. These brands are overcompensating on the acidity in order to combat the effects of oxygen on the avocados, and resultantly, they completely destroy the flavor. I cannot remember the name of the cookbook we get our measurements from, but it is the one in our house with tattered pages, extreme tomato juice damage and avocado stains. You will notice how no where in my ingredient list did I mention sour cream or greek yogurt. These do NOT belong in guacamole. Avocados are literal balls of creamy, healthy fat and there is ABSOLUTELY no need to be adding those.

Now, in terms of the chips, if you want to have an amazing guacamole experience, you need to be scooping it up with tostada chips. In our house, tostada chips entails cutting up flour tortillas into triangles, frying those in a vat of oil, then making sure they have a generous sprinkle of salt before serving hot. I say that I only ~partially~ make these because I have no involvement in the actual tortilla making. I have to credit Central Market who quite literally make the tastiest, fluffiest, most velvety flour tortillas I have ever had. Central Market does all the work, we just add that extra infusion of fat and salt which takes ‘em to a whole ‘nother level. In case you haven’t realized, these chips served by guacamole made my way is my favorite meal.

To run fast, turn into a cheetah. To hold your breath, turn into a fish. My favorite shows are Game of Thrones, Rick and Morty, Chef’s Table and Parks and Recreation.
